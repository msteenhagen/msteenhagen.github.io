Mind-reading

Mon, Nov 5, 2018 Part II, Philosophy of Mind, Michaelmas 2018

Course description

These lectures will introduce the problem of other minds and discuss the philosophical debate about mind-reading.

Online resources

Outlines and handouts will be made available on:

http://msteenhagen.github.io/teaching/2018mre/

Where and when

Monday 1-2pm. Lecture Block Room 12.

Lecture 1: Sympathy, empathy and the problem of other minds

This lecture will introduce a history of thinking about empathy and the problem of other minds. A central argument in this discussion is the Argument from Analogy. How good is this argument?

Lecture 2: Theory theory

This lecture will critically examine the ‘Theory Theory’. Some philosophers and psychologists believe that we (and possibly other animals) use a Theory of Mind (ToM) to represent and predict the mental states of others. The theory in question is a form of folk psychology, and some suggest we use this theory both to make sense of the mental states of others as well as of our own mental states.

(Download handout here)

Lecture 3: Simulation theory

This lecture will introduce the discussion about Simulation Theory. This view holds that our knowledge of other minds is fundamentally attained through acts of mental simulation: imaginative projects in which we pretend to take up the perspective of the other. A particular focus will be on the work of Jane Heal.

(Download handout here)

Lecture 4: Empathy

In this lecture we will discuss non-reductionist approaches to mind-reading. We will examine a reading of Hume that allows the mental states of others to be directly present to us, and consider a more recent proposal by Edith Stein, according to which empathy is an intentional mode in its own right.
