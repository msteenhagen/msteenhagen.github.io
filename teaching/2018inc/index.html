Induction and Causation

Fri, Nov 2, 2018 Part Ia, Metaphysics, Michaelmas 2018

Course description

These lectures will introduce the problems of induction and the metaphysics of causation (or causality, or cause and effect).

In this lecture I introduce the idea of induction, and discuss David Hume’s problem of induction.

Lecture 3: Causes and regularities

In this lecture I introduce David Humeâ€™s sceptical approach to causation, and show how it has led the way for regularity theories of causation. There are some problems with such theories. In particular, regularity theories seem to have little to say about the particular events that seem to be fundamental to causal interaction.

J.L. Mackie offers an analysis that identifies these particular events as special conditions: INUS conditions. His suggestion is that something is a cause if it is at least an INUS condition for an effect. I close by discussing three problems with Mackie’s theory.

(Download handout here)

Lecture 4: Counterfactual theories

In this lecture I introduce David Lewis’ framework for thinking about counterfactual conditionals, and show how he applies this framework to a theory of causation. Causal dependence is a relation between distinct events that is defined in terms of counterfactual dependence relations between statements about these events’ occurrence (or absence thereof).

The counterfactual theory gives rise to question baout the asymmetry and transitivity of causation, and does not always deal well with cases of overdetermination and pre-emption.
