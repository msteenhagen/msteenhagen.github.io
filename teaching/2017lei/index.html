Leibniz

Tue, Oct 10, 2017 Part 1b, Early Modern Philosophy, Michaelmas 2017

Course description

Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (1646, Leipzig â€“ 1716, Hanover) was one of the last Renaissance intellectuals (‘a universal mind’). He made lasting contributions to philosophy, mathematics, theology, jurisprudence, politics, technology, and architecture. This lecture course will offer an overview of some central ideas in his philosophy. Feel free to contact me at ms2416@cam.ac.uk if you have any suggestions, questions, or comments about this course.
